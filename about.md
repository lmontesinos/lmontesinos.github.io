Luis Montesinos is a biomedical engineering researcher and educator whose work integrates biomedical signal processing, machine learning, human-centered systems, and sustainable healthcare.

His research interests include health data science, physiological and behavioral signal analysis, wearable technologies, human performance, ergonomics, extended reality for training and simulation, and the environmental sustainability of healthcare systems and technologies.

Across his work, he is interested in how data, technology, and engineering methods can support better decision-making in health, work, education, and complex operational environments.

## Areas of expertise

- Biomedical signal processing
- Machine learning and data science in medicine
- Wearable systems for physiological and behavioral monitoring
- Human factors, ergonomics, and human performance
- Extended reality and simulation-based training
- Sustainable healthcare and circular economy approaches
- Engineering education and experiential learning
