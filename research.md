## Biomedical signal processing and machine learning in medicine

This line focuses on the acquisition, analysis, and interpretation of biomedical signals and clinical data. It includes applications of machine learning for risk classification, pattern recognition, and decision support in health-related contexts.

Representative interests include:

- Signal processing for physiological and clinical data
- Machine learning for disease risk assessment and outcome prediction
- Fall-risk classification and balance assessment
- Health data science workflows for clinical and biomedical applications

## Wearable systems, ergonomics, and human performance

This line addresses the use of wearable devices and quantitative methods to evaluate physical, physiological, and behavioral aspects of human performance.

Representative interests include:

- Wearable physiological monitoring
- Ergonomic and biomechanical assessment
- Worker safety, health, and performance
- Physiological and behavioral indicators of workload, fatigue, and stress

## Sustainable healthcare and circularity

This line explores how engineering, environmental assessment, and systems thinking can contribute to more sustainable healthcare practices and technologies.

Representative interests include:

- Sustainable operating rooms
- Life cycle assessment of healthcare procedures and technologies
- Circular economy approaches for medical devices and materials
- Waste management frameworks in clinical environments

## Extended reality, digital twins, and human-centered systems

This line investigates the use of virtual reality, extended reality, digital twins, and AI-enabled environments for training, decision support, and complex scenario simulation.

Representative interests include:

- Extended reality training for complex scenarios
- Human digital twins and physiological response modeling
- Human-machine-environment interaction
- Simulation-based decision support

## Engineering education and experiential learning

This line examines how active, experiential, and technology-enhanced learning environments can support engineering education.

Representative interests include:

- Challenge-based and research-based learning
- Virtual reality and AI tools in education
- Learning outcomes assessment
- Student engagement and motivation
